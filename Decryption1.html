B-3.3 Decryption

1.
Given the ciphertext (A,B) where B = A ⋅S + Δ ⋅M + E ∈R⟨n,q⟩, compute B −A ⋅S = Δ ⋅M + E.
2.
Round each coefficient of the polynomial Δ ⋅M + E ∈R⟨n,q⟩ to the nearest multiple of Δ (i.e., round it as a base Δ number), which is denoted as ⌈Δ ⋅M + E⌋Δ. This rounding operation successfully eliminates E and gives Δ ⋅M. One caveat is that the noise E’s each coefficient ei should be small enough to be ei < Δ 2 in order to be eliminated during the rounding. Otherwise, some of ei’s higher bits will overlap and corrupt the plaintext mi coefficient’s lower bits and won’t be blown away.
3.
Compute Δ ⋅M Δ , which is equivalent to right-shifting each polynomial coefficient in Δ ⋅M by log2Δ bits.

For correct decryption, every noise coefficient ei of polynomial E should be: ei < Δ 2. And in case t does not divide q, q should be sufficiently larger than t.
