B-2.2 Encryption

1.
Suppose we have a plaintext m ∈ ℤt to encrypt.
2.
Randomly pick a vector A ∈ ℤqk (of length k) as a one-time public key (denoted as A ←$ℤqk).
3.
Randomly pick a small one-time noise e ∈ ℤq sampled from the Gaussian distribution χσ (denoted as e ←χσℤq).
4.
Scale m by Δ, which is to compute Δ ⋅m. This converts m ∈ ℤt into Δ ⋅m ∈ ℤq.
5.
Compute b = A ⋅S + Δ ⋅m + e ∈ ℤq.
