A-3.3 Theorems

⟨Theorem A-3.3⟩ Field Theorems

1.
Size of Finite Field: Every finite field is called a Galois Field and it has pn elements for some prime p and positive integer n, conversely, for each pn there exists a finite field of that size (unique up to isomorphism).
2.
Isomorphic Fields: Any two finite fields 𝔽1 and 𝔽2 with the same number of elements are isomorphic, i.e., there exists a bijection f : 𝔽1 → 𝔽2 such that for all a,b ∈ 𝔽1, f(a + b) = f(a) + f(b) and f(𝑎𝑏) = f(a)f(b).
